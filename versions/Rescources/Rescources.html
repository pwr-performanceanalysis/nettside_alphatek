Nyquist 2023 – Velocity Loss Thresholds in Hockey

In-season hockey athletes training to 40% velocity loss demonstrated greater leg-press strength gains compared to 20%, although muscle size increases remained similar.

Remme 2023 – Power Development & Velocity Loss

Training to 40% velocity loss led to increased lower-limb maximal power output; however, jump and sprint performances were unaffected compared to 20% velocity loss.

Vormeland Paulsen 2024 – Feedback Modes

Color-coded velocity-loss feedback ("traffic-light" system) enabled higher lifting velocity, about 20% more repetitions per set, and significant improvements in squat strength and jump height compared to velocity-only feedback.

Bjørnsen 2024 – Color-coded Feedback in-Season

Using the traffic-light velocity-loss system helped athletes maintain higher lifting velocities across repetitions, resulting in enhanced season-long strength and power gains.

Bjelland 2024 – Readiness and Autoregulation

Combining subjective readiness assessments with velocity metrics best predicted daily training performance; notably, training autoregulation was more effective at 30% velocity loss than at 20%.
